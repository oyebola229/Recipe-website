Ingredients

  • Afang leaves (okazi leaves), finely chopped
  • Waterleaf, finely chopped
  • Assorted meat (beef, goat meat, or tripe)
  • Cow skin (ponmo), cut into bite-sized pieces
  • Smoked fish, deboned and flaked
  • Periwinkles (optional)
  • Onion, chopped
  • Scotch bonnet peppers (ata rodo), chopped
  • Ground crayfish
  • Seasoning cubes or powder
  • Palm oil
  • Salt to taste

Instructions

  1. Wash and season the assorted meat and cow skin with salt, chopped onion, and seasoning cubes or powder. Cook until tender, then set aside.
  2. In a large pot, heat the palm oil over medium heat. Add the chopped Scotch bonnet peppers and ground crayfish, and stir-fry for a few minutes.
  3. Add the cooked meat, cow skin, and smoked fish to the pot. Stir to combine with the oil and spices.
  4. Gradually add the chopped waterleaf, stirring as you go. Allow the waterleaf to release its moisture and cook down.
  5. Once the waterleaf has reduced, add the finely chopped Afang leaves. Stir well to combine.
  6. Add the periwinkles (if using) and adjust the seasoning with more salt or seasoning cubes or powder if needed.
  7. Simmer the soup for an additional 10-15 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.
  8. Once the soup is well-cooked and the leaves are tender, remove from heat.
  9. Serve the Afang Soup hot with pounded yam, fufu, garri, or your preferred side dish.
