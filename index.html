Jollof Rice

A West African staple, Jollof rice is a vibrant one-pot dish made with rice, tomatoes, onions, and a blend of spices. Often served with fried plantains, grilled chicken, or fish, it’s a must-have at Nigerian parties and gatherings.

Egusi Soup

Egusi soup is a thick, hearty soup made from ground melon seeds (egusi), leafy vegetables, and assorted meats. It’s usually enjoyed with pounded yam, fufu, or eba

Moi-Moi

Moi Moi is a savory steamed bean pudding made from peeled black-eyed peas, onions, and peppers. It’s a popular side dish in Nigeria, often served with jollof rice or on its own.

Pepper Soup

Nigerian Pepper Soup is a spicy, broth-based dish made with a variety of meats or fish, and a blend of aromatic spices. It’s often served as an appetizer or a light meal.

Afang Soup

Afang soup is a delicious and nutritious vegetable soup made with Afang leaves (wild spinach) and waterleaf. It’s a popular dish among the Efik and Ibibio people of Southern Nigeria. Rich in flavor and loaded with assorted meats, it’s typically served with fufu, eba, or pounded yam.

Akara-Beancake

Akara are deep-fried bean cakes made from black-eyed peas, onions, and spices. They are crispy on the outside and soft on the inside, often enjoyed as breakfast with pap (ogi) or bread.
